Zhixiang Cheng

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the school of computer science, HNU. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. X. Zeng. I obtained my B.E. degree in data science and big data technology at TYUT in 2022.

Research Interest: My research interests focus on AI4Science.

  • AI4Science: 1) Drug Discovery, particularly drug property prediction and protein representation learning with vision methods; 2) Materials Discovery, with more focus on physics-informed machine learning.

Publication & Activities: Most of my research papers have been published in interdisciplinary journals and computer science conferences, such as BMC biology, IEEE JBHI, IEEE TCBB, JMC, NeurIPS. A full publication list can be found at google scholar. I also serve as reviewer in

  • Journals: Molecular Therapy.

news

2026 🎉 Our work VisionED (Physics-Informed Self-Supervised Learning on Efficient Electron-Density Images for Organic Material Property Prediction) will be online. We introduce VisionED, a physics-informed framework for organic material property prediction based on efficient multi-shot electron-density (ED) images rendered from DFT calculations.
Mar 2, 2026 🎉 Our work ReMol (A Chemical Reaction Knowledge-guided Self-supervised Molecular Image Representation Learning Framework) is accepted to IEEE JBHI. The ReMol framework integrates comprehensive reaction inductive biases, including reaction templates and consistency, and diversity in chemical reactions for chemical reaction and molecular property prediction tasks.
Sep 24, 2025 🎉 Our work MaskMol (knowledge-guided molecular image pre-training framework for activity cliffs with pixel masking) is accepted to BMC Biology. We developed MaskMol, a knowledge-guided molecular image self-supervised learning framework for activity cliff estimation. By utilizing pixel masking tasks, MaskMol extracts fine-grained information from molecular images, overcoming the limitations of existing deep learning models in identifying subtle structural changes.
Sep 19, 2025 🎉 Our work EDBench (Large-Scale Electron Density Data for Molecular Modeling) is accepted to NeurIPS 2025. In this paper, we introduce EDBench, a large-scale, high-quality dataset of ED designed to advance learning-based research at the electronic scale. Built upon the PCQM4Mv2, EDBench provides accurate ED data, covering 3.3 million molecules.
